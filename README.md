## 🍽️ In Lieu of a Methods Section: A Recipe

*Since we're not giving anything away, here is the only methodology we're prepared to share publicly.*

### Blood Sugar Roller Coaster Cookies
*Yield: ~24 cookies | Prep time: 15 min | Spike time: ~30 min post-consumption*

**Ingredients**
- 200 g white flour *(high glycaemic index — for authenticity)*
- 150 g white sugar *(see above)*
- 125 g butter, softened *(the good kind)*
- 1 egg
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- ½ tsp baking soda
- 1 pinch of salt *(a nod to the bitter reality of academic publishing timelines)*
- A handful of chocolate chips *(optional, but strongly recommended — p < 0.001)*

**Instructions**

1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Cream butter and sugar until fluffy — much like massaging your data until the reviewers are satisfied.
2. Beat in the egg and vanilla. Do not over-mix. Over-mixing leads to tough cookies and, by analogy, over-fitted models.
3. Fold in flour, baking soda, and salt. Add chocolate chips. Resist eating the dough; this is a negative control.
4. Drop spoonfuls onto a baking tray. Note the between-batch variability in cookie size. This is normal. This is life.
5. Bake for 10–12 minutes until golden. Remove before they are *too* significant — a little uncertainty is healthy.
6. Allow to cool. Consume. Observe your own postprandial glycaemic response. Consider wearing a CGM for full experimental rigour.

**Notes:** Results may vary depending on oven calibration, butter fat content, and whether you ate lunch. Individual responses are expected. That's kind of the whole point of the paper.
